What to Pack

You will need a bunch of stuff if you want to overnight in Harriman, but there is good news: there are free, no-reservations required shelters around the park. That being said, some leak, some have mice, some have ticks, and some have other stinky hikers in them. So I'd recommend bringing a tent. Here's what I packed: Think of it as bringing different rooms
  • Bedroom: tent, sleeping bag, sleeping pad (yes you need this), lantern, camp pillow (optional)
  • Kitchen: portable cook stove, fuel, instant meals, spork, water bottle, water filter, collapsible bowl, bear bag with paracord and carabiner
  • Bathroom: first aid kit, trowel, tp, baby wipes (in place of a shower)
  • 'Vehicle': backpack, hiking shoes, wool socks, poles, headlamp, snacks, 2l water bladder with hose
All of this stuff, when chosen wisely, can fit in a 40 liter pack that weighs about 16lb and is good for up to 3 nights.
