Bringing the Kids

Mia is 6 and just recently decided she wanted to do an overnight, so we packed her pack very light (she was in charge of snacks and marshmallows). Ray is 9 so he got a 30 liter backpack with a water bladder and a sleeping bag, lights, water filter. Adults were in charge of other sleeping bags, the tent, pads, cooking stove and dehydrated food. It was a regular coleman 6 person tent so very heavy! Our light backpacking tent only sleeps 2 and we didn’t want to buy a bunch more stuff just for the trip.

I am happy to report this was 100% legit primitive camping, nothing for you but a patch of dirt where you could pitch a tent!

In order to plan, I took an overnight backpacking course with REI, and learned some things I wouldn’t have thought of. Then I tried that same route (Tuxedo station to Dutch Doctor shelter, about a 3 mile hike) without the kids , and realized it would be fine to bring them. Then I made a packing list and did a practice run of what would go in who’s packs, thinking about a weight limit of 10-20% body weight. We also brought lots of snacks and easy food like cup noodles, not to mention smores kits and uno for the kids! Basically... if you can keep the kids happy and comfortable it will be a success!

Bringing the kids can make lasting memories for the whole family! Positive memories, even, if you plan right!
