Solo Overnight

Hey, I know it isn't exactly summer anymore, but I got my gear and it's burning a hole in my closet! I gotta go!

This through trek started at the Southfields Coach USA stop.

I hiked up the Nurian trail, with a rickety scary wooden bridge over the Ramapo river, and a solid scary pedestrian overpass over I-87.

Today my destination was Bald Rocks, a shelter 6 miles uphill. With exactly 6 hours of daylight left, I figured I would meet my pace of a mile an hour and get set for camp just as the sun was setting.

Camping in Harriman is 100% primitive. What does that mean? Nothing, not even water, not even toilets, nothing! is provided.
There are 9 lean-to's around the park, and staying in them overnight is on a first-come, first-serve basis.
You may also pitch a tent within 150' of any of these shelters.
Anywhere else is technically illegal, so don't risk it.
Here is the list of shelters:

  • Dutch Doctor
  • Tom Jones
  • Bald Rocks
  • Big Hill
  • Fingerboard
Note: there are bears in the park. I have seen one personally. It was small and walked away as soon as it saw me, but I would not trust it to stay out of my tent if there were food in it. Make sure to bring a bear bag and know how to hang it. There are cables with pre-tied carabiners for you to mount your bags by most shelters.

You will need a bunch of stuff if you want to overnight in Harriman, but there is good news: there are free, no-reservations required shelters around the park. That being said, some leak, some have mice, some have ticks, and some have other stinky hikers in them. So I'd recommend bringing a tent. Here's what I packed: Think of it as bringing different rooms.

  • Bedroom: tent, sleeping bag, sleeping pad (yes you need this), lantern, camp pillow (optional)
  • Kitchen: portable cook stove, fuel, instant meals, spork, water bottle, water filter, collapsible bowl, bear bag with paracord and carabiner
  • Bathroom: first aid kit, trowel, tp, baby wipes (in place of a shower)
  • 'Vehicle': backpack, hiking shoes, wool socks, poles, headlamp, snacks, 2l water bladder with hose
All of this stuff, when chosen wisely, can fit in a 40 liter pack that weighs about 16lb and is good for up to 3 nights.

Solo overnights make you feel so connected to nature!
